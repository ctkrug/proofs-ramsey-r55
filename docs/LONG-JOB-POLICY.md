# Checkpointed computation policy

Substantial computation begins only after an efficiency-design pass records the naive cost, considered reductions, chosen mechanisms, expected throughput, shortcut soundness, and the space left uncompressed. A measured pilot establishes actual throughput and artifact growth.

There is no universal overall wall-clock cutoff. Each segment remains resource- and time-bounded. Continuation requires a predeclared decisive result or reusable artifact, immutable inputs and revision, atomic checkpoints, durable progress, passing correctness controls, acceptable measured throughput and artifact growth, and a completion event that survives model scheduling gaps. At every pilot or review tranche the job enters `completed_awaiting_review`; a later evidence-backed reviewer chooses `continue`, `validate`, `promote`, or `redirect`.

The R(5,5) supplied-class census is permitted at its current approximately 5.6 core-hour estimate. It covers all 328 supplied graphs and their 328 complements with two enumerators and compressed per-host receipts. Before any expansion beyond that full supplied corpus, the route must pass a bounded residual-SAT discriminator and a materially independent validation.
